Biography of Governor Kevin Stitt

Stitt has been serving as the 28th Governor of Oklahoma since January 2019. Born on December 28, 1972, in Milton, Florida, Stitt moved to Oklahoma at a young age, eventually graduating from Oklahoma State University with a degree in accounting.

Early Life and Education

Kevin Stitt was born to a family of Cherokee descent and moved to Skiatook, Oklahoma, when he was five. He attended Norman High School and later Oklahoma State University, where he earned a degree in accounting. To finance his education, Stitt sold educational products door-to-door, becoming the first person in Southwestern Advantage's history to achieve top sales as a first-year salesperson.

Business Career

In 2000, Stitt founded Gateway Mortgage Group with just $1,000 and a computer. Under his leadership, the company grew into a nationwide mortgage provider, operating in 42 states and servicing over $20 billion in residential mortgages. By 2018, Gateway had merged to form Gateway First Bank, now one of Oklahoma's 10 largest banks by assets, with over $2 billion in assets and more than 1,600 employees.

Political Career

Stitt entered politics with a vision to make Oklahoma a Top Ten state in job growth, infrastructure, and education. In the 2018 gubernatorial election, he defeated Democrat Drew Edmondson, receiving more votes than any gubernatorial candidate in Oklahoma's history at the time. He was re-elected in 2022, defeating Joy Hofmeister.

As governor, Stitt has appointed a historically high number of female cabinet secretaries and integrated private sector experts into his administration. His leadership style emphasizes fiscal conservatism and business acumen, which has helped Oklahoma build its largest savings account in history.

Personal Life

Stitt is a fourth-generation Oklahoman and a member of the Cherokee Nation. He and his wife, Sarah, have six children. They are active members of Woodlake Church in Tulsa.
